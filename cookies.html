Cookie Policy

How we use cookies and similar technologies to improve your experience.

Last Updated: March 14, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how Brillix Technologies uses them on our website, and what options you have to control them. This policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy, which provides further details on how we collect and process your personal data.

What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used across the internet to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and supply information to site owners. Cookies are stored in your browser and allow a website to recognise your device and remember certain information about your visit, such as your preferences and past actions.

Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your device after you close your browser and are activated each time you revisit the website that created them. Session cookies are temporary and are deleted from your device once you close your browser. Both types serve different purposes in enabling website functionality and analytics.

Types of Cookies We Use

Our website uses the following categories of cookies, each serving a specific purpose:

  • Essential Cookies: These cookies are strictly necessary for the operation of our website. They enable core functionality such as session management, security features, and basic navigation. Without these cookies, the website cannot function properly. Essential cookies do not collect personal information and cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics Cookies: We use Google Analytics cookies to collect anonymised information about how visitors use our website. These cookies help us understand which pages are visited most frequently, how long users spend on each page, and where visitors come from. The data collected is aggregated and does not identify individual users.
  • Functional Cookies: These cookies allow our website to remember choices you make, such as your preferred language, region, or display settings. They enhance your experience by providing personalised features and remembering your preferences for future visits.
  • Performance Cookies: Performance cookies collect information about how our website performs, including page load times, error messages, and response times. This data helps us identify and resolve technical issues, optimise page speed, and ensure our website delivers a smooth browsing experience across all devices.

How We Use Cookies

Cookies play an important role in helping us deliver a reliable and user-friendly website. We use the information gathered through cookies for the following purposes:

  • Improving User Experience: Cookies allow us to remember your preferences and settings, so you do not have to re-enter them each time you visit. This creates a more seamless and personalised browsing experience.
  • Understanding Usage Patterns: Analytics cookies provide us with valuable insights into how visitors interact with our website. We use this data to identify popular content, understand navigation patterns, and make informed decisions about site improvements and content strategy.
  • Remembering Preferences: Functional cookies store your choices and preferences, ensuring that your experience is consistent across sessions. This includes settings such as display preferences and any customisations you have made.
  • Ensuring Performance: Performance cookies help us monitor the technical health of our website, enabling us to detect and resolve issues quickly to maintain optimal loading speeds and reliability.

Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, we use cookies set by trusted third-party services that are integrated into our website. These third parties have their own privacy and cookie policies, and we encourage you to review them:

  • Google Analytics: Google Analytics places cookies on your device to collect anonymised usage data. This helps us understand visitor behaviour and improve our website. Google processes this data in accordance with its own privacy policy, and you can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.
  • CDN Providers: We use content delivery networks (CDNs) to serve fonts, JavaScript libraries, and other static resources. These providers may set cookies or collect technical data such as your IP address as part of delivering content efficiently to your browser.
  • EmailJS: Our contact form is powered by EmailJS, which may use cookies to process form submissions and prevent spam. EmailJS handles data in accordance with its own privacy policy.

Managing Cookies

You have full control over which cookies are stored on your device. Most web browsers allow you to manage cookie settings through their preferences or settings menu. You can choose to block all cookies, accept only certain types, or delete cookies that have already been stored.

Here is how to manage cookies in the most popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Go to Settings, then Privacy and Security, then Cookies and other site data. From there, you can block third-party cookies, clear existing cookies, or manage exceptions for specific websites.
  • Mozilla Firefox: Open Settings, navigate to Privacy & Security, and look for the Cookies and Site Data section. Firefox offers Enhanced Tracking Protection which automatically blocks many third-party tracking cookies.
  • Apple Safari: Go to Preferences, then Privacy. Safari provides options to block all cookies, prevent cross-site tracking, and manage website data. Safari blocks third-party cookies by default.
  • Microsoft Edge: Open Settings, go to Cookies and site permissions, then Manage and delete cookies and site data. You can choose to block third-party cookies, clear cookies on exit, or add exceptions.

Please note that clearing or blocking cookies may affect your experience on other websites as well, not just ours. Browser cookie settings apply globally to all websites you visit.

Impact of Disabling Cookies

While you have the right to disable cookies, please be aware that doing so may impact the functionality of our website. If essential cookies are blocked, certain features of the site may not work as intended, and you may experience issues with navigation or form submissions.

Disabling analytics and performance cookies will not affect the core functionality of our website, but it will prevent us from collecting data that helps us improve the site. Blocking functional cookies may mean that your preferences are not remembered between visits, requiring you to reconfigure settings each time.

When changes are made, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to check this page periodically to stay informed about how we use cookies.

Any significant changes to our cookie practices will also be communicated through a notice on our website. Your continued use of the website after any updates to this policy constitutes your acceptance of the revised terms.
